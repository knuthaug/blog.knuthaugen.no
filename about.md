<h2>About the author</h2>

<p>Well hello there, I'm Knut Haugen [Knu:t Hæugen] a Norwegian software developer with a soft spot for dynamic languages. I started my career in software development in 2000 during the throws of the dotcom bubble, which admittedly wasn't as bad here in Norway compared to some other places, but it was pretty wild at times. I spent the first years working as a Linux consultant doing sysadmin stuff and programming Perl for a living. Interesting times it was. I blame my now late uncle for setting me on that course. When I bought my first own computer in 1997 just before moving to study information technology, he came over with a double CD set with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FreeBSD">FreeBSD</a>2.2.6 and running that as your first OS (ok, I had a Windows dual boot) does something with you. Over the years I have developed a severe windows allergy and it is 100% unthinkable to work on Windows now. 
</p>

<p>After that I worked a couple of years in law enforcement doing system forensics trying to get financial criminals behind bars. Got recruited to a software development company after that, but it was a really shitty company where I only stayed for a year before moving over to media. First tenure in Amedia was spent doing cloud and kubernetes automation and backend work. Last 8 years now have been spent in the public sector with the Norwegian National Broadcaster working on streaming video players and infrastructure. So i started on linux and OS and have ended up in the frontend of the stack doing web development. My main focus these days is browser performance and I have been lucky enough to get to speak about that topic at a couple of places, first and foremost on <a href="https://2025.javazone.no/en/program/1b3cf4b2-7987-4cd0-a346-24c6063e8d4d?__EXPO_ROUTER_key=undefined-TcCTe-By5AwmC4_5LWDk8">JavaZone 2025</a> (in Norwegian)</p>
